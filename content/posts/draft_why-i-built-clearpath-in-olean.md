# Why I built ClearPath in Olean

Most of the AI consulting you read about online is built for companies with hundreds of employees and an IT department. The pitch decks are sleek. The pricing is six figures. The advice is mostly translated from "we built this for Fortune 500" with a few small-business words swapped in.

That's not what runs the businesses I grew up around.

I'm Justin. I grew up in Western New York. I spent twelve-plus years on the operations side of Holiday Valley Resort in Ellicottville — back of house at Edna's, slinging drinks at the Cabana Bar, teaching ski school. Real work. Long shifts. Local people. The math of running that kind of operation isn't theoretical to me. It's what staffing the Saturday rush looks like when two people called out and there's a wedding party walking in at four.

A few years into the ski school job, we hired a new director. Sharp guy, came up through the race world, knew skiing inside and out — but the resort had a few hundred quirks he had no way of knowing on day one. Pricing variations for half-day vs. full-day, age-bracket policies, how rentals tied into lessons, which staff member to ask about which thing. Every time he had a question, he'd ping someone, and that someone was probably mid-lesson with a customer's kid.

So I built him a chatbot. Took the resort's old training docs and a few thousand Slack threads, fed them into an AI tool, gave it a name, and pointed him at it. He could ask "what's the price for a four-year-old beginner snowboard lesson on a Saturday?" and get an answer in five seconds without interrupting an instructor. Took a weekend to set up. The instructors got hours of their day back.

That same year, I was teaching enough private lessons that guests started asking for me by name. Problem was, the resort's booking flow for private lessons routed everything through the front desk — and the front desk, on a busy Saturday, can take three hours to call you back. By the time they did, the customer had moved on or the slot was gone.

So I built a "book with Justin" website. Dead simple. Three time slots, a Stripe checkout, a confirmation email. The customer could book a Tuesday-at-noon private lesson on a Wednesday night without waiting on a callback. I'd see the booking in my email, drive to the resort, and teach the lesson. Nobody at the resort had to lift a finger.

Both of those started as side projects to scratch my own itch. Both of them surfaced the same lesson: the small specific fixes that save five hours a week add up faster than you'd think. Nobody in either case needed a new platform. They needed someone willing to look at the actual workflow and say "this part shouldn't take this long."

That's what ClearPath is.

I sit down with a small business owner in Olean, or wider Western New York, and I walk through how a typical week runs. Where the boring repetitive work lives. Where the same email gets retyped twenty times. Where the customer follow-up keeps slipping because nobody owns it. Where someone's exporting a CSV from one system every Monday morning to paste into another. The audit is a 45-minute discovery call and a 2-3 page report. No deck. No buzzwords. No "we'll circle back on the synergies."

The tools I recommend are usually things you already pay for. Gmail. Outlook. Microsoft 365. Google Workspace. Calendly. The point isn't to add another vendor — it's to turn on the parts of what you have that you didn't know existed.

Why Olean specifically? Because I'm not trying to be a national consultancy. I'm trying to be the local guy who knows what running a real Western New York small business actually looks like — and who can show you, on your screen, how a fix works rather than tell you about it in a slide deck. Most of the AI consulting in the news is aimed at companies ten times your size. I built ClearPath for the cafe down the street and the auto shop near Bradford and the CPA office in Cattaraugus County, because that's where the unfair advantage is — not in a Buffalo high-rise.

A real client called me his "let-me-show-you person" once. That's the job. Not consulting. Not pitching. Just showing you, on your screen, how something works — so when I'm gone, you keep running it yourself.

If any of that sounds like your business, the first step is a 45-minute call. No prep needed. No pitch. We'll talk about how a typical week runs, and I'll tell you whether there's anything here for you. If there isn't, I'll say so.

— Justin
